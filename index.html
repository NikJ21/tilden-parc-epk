About Tilden Parc

Tilden Parc is an Oakland-based rapper and producer known for his unique blend of hip-hop and innovative production. With a distinctive sound that captures the essence of the Bay Area, Tilden Parc has established himself as a rising force in the music industry.

His latest releases showcase his evolution as an artist, combining raw lyricism with sophisticated production techniques that push the boundaries of contemporary hip-hop.

Born in Richmond, CA to a Louisiana Creole family, his music journey began in a church before evolving into a globally streamed career. He's collaborated with Katori Walker, King Sis, Xavy Rusan, Iso Indies, Heyson, Fijiana, and many more.

As an independent Black American rapper & producer, Tilden Parc is known for his potent pen, soulful delivery, and futuristic production that blends Bay Area roots with trunk-rattling Conscious Trap and R&B.

His recent singles, Rare Form and Fire, showcase airtight flows and quotable bars, building off a catalog that includes WORK IT OUT, MISS AMERICA, and Word Is Bond. Tilden Parc makes melodic, propulsive rap music that evokes the sensation of fluid forward motion.

A charismatic vocalist, sharp writer, and versatile producer, he combines bass-heavy production styles with potent wordplay to explore ideas and stories about channeling self-belief and championing mind over matter to overcome everyday struggles. These themes are rooted in his pursuit of a full and dynamic life.

New Music Spotlight: Grizzly Peak Rd

Debut R&B Single

As a Rapper and Producer stepping into the Contemporary R&B lane this track is a declaration for me. It's rhythmic precision and sensual storytelling. It's not just a shift in sound. It's a statement of range and intention. It's a celebration of love and reverence for the lineage of both genres.

Lyrically, the song explores intimacy through metaphors like "I turn yo river to an ocean" and "I learn them waters like the back of my hand," suggesting not just physical closeness, but emotional mastery and connection. Repetition in the hook becomes a hypnotic mantra rather than a refrain, grounding the song in rhythm and desire.

Grizzly Peak is for the grown and sexy through modern romance. It's thematically written with Aaliyah's Rock the Boat as a prequel, capturing the longing and anticipation that precedes full intimacy. The pitched-down vocal intro is a nod to Rob Base's It Takes Two.

This one creates a subtle salute to the relationship between hip-hop & traditional Contemporary R&B.
